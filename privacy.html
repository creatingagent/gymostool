Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026 · Applies to gymostool.xyz and associated services

The short version: We collect the minimum data needed to run GymOS — gym details, member phone numbers from Stripe, and message logs. We never sell your data. We never touch your money. You can delete everything by emailing us.

1. Who we are

GymOS is a payment recovery service operated by Meraki ("we", "us", "our"). Our contact email is gymostool@gmail.com.

For the purposes of EU data protection law, we act as a data controller for the personal data of gym owners and as a data processor for the personal data of gym members (processed on behalf of the gym owner, who is the controller).

2. What data we collect

From gym owners (our direct customers)

  • Name, business email, WhatsApp number
  • Gym name, country, number of members
  • Stripe Account ID (used only to connect our service to your payments)
  • Payment records (managed via Stripe; we do not store card data)

From gym members (via your Stripe integration)

  • Phone number (to send WhatsApp recovery messages)
  • Invoice status (to determine whether to send a message)
  • We do not collect names, email addresses or card details of gym members unless passed to us explicitly

Usage and technical data

  • IP address and browser type (standard web server logs, retained for 30 days)
  • Pages visited on gymostool.xyz
  • Message delivery logs (timestamp, recipient number, delivery status)

3. How we use your data

  • To deliver the service: detecting failed payments, sending WhatsApp recovery messages, logging outcomes
  • To communicate with you: onboarding, support, billing notifications
  • To improve the service: aggregated, anonymised usage analysis
  • To comply with legal obligations: including the EU AI Act (Article 50 transparency), GDPR, and tax/financial regulations

4. Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

  • Contract performance — processing necessary to deliver the GymOS service you signed up for (Art. 6(1)(b))
  • Legitimate interests — service improvement, fraud prevention (Art. 6(1)(f))
  • Legal obligation — compliance with applicable EU law (Art. 6(1)(c))

5. Who we share data with

We do not sell personal data. We share data only with:

  • Stripe — payment processing and invoice status checks (Stripe Privacy Policy)
  • Meta / WhatsApp Business API — delivery of recovery messages (Meta Privacy Policy)
  • Supabase — database hosting (EU servers) (Supabase Privacy Policy)
  • Render — application hosting (Render Privacy Policy)
  • Google — form submissions and analytics (Google Privacy Policy)

6. Data retention

  • Active customer data: retained while your subscription is active
  • Message logs: 12 months from send date
  • Billing records: 7 years (legal requirement)
  • After account closure: all personal data deleted within 30 days, except billing records

7. Your rights (GDPR)

If you are in the EU or UK, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Portability — receive your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent)

To exercise any of these rights, email gymostool@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority (in Spain: AEPD).

8. Cookies

We use minimal cookies. See our Cookie Policy for details.

9. Security

All data is transmitted over TLS/HTTPS. We do not store Stripe secret keys, card numbers or CVVs. Access to our database is protected by Row Level Security. We conduct regular security reviews.

10. International transfers

Our hosting is in the EU (Frankfurt). Where data is processed outside the EU (e.g. via Meta's servers), we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions.

11. Children

GymOS is a B2B service intended for businesses. We do not knowingly collect data from individuals under 18.

12. AI disclosure (EU AI Act, Article 50)

GymOS uses automated systems to detect failed payments and generate WhatsApp messages. In compliance with the EU AI Act (effective 2 August 2026), every message sent to gym members by our system includes the disclosure: "This is an automated message from [Gym Name]."

Our system is classified as low-risk under the EU AI Act. It performs transactional notifications only and is not used for hiring, biometric identification, or any high-risk purpose.

We do not use gym member data to train AI models.

13. Changes to this policy

We will notify active customers by email of any material changes. The date at the top of this page always reflects the most recent revision.
